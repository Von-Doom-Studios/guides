# Brand Voice — Floki

This document defines how Floki communicates. It applies to the website, social media, email, ads, and any other customer-facing content.

---

## Core Tone

**Premium. Confident. Minimal.**

Floki is not a discount brand. The copy should feel like the product looks — considered, clean, worth it.

---

## Rules

- **Short sentences.** No run-ons. No fluff.
- **No filler words.** Cut "incredibly," "amazing," "truly," "really," "just."
- **No exclamation marks.** Ever.
- **No clichés.** "Elevate your life," "luxury redefined," "where quality meets style" — all banned.
- **No corporate speak.** Write like a person, not a press release.
- **Never salesy.** State facts and let the product sell itself.

---

## Target Customer

Style-conscious, quality-aware. Ages 25–45. They notice details. They buy things once and buy them right.

---

## Voice in Practice

| Instead of... | Write... |
|---|---|
| "Elevate your home with our amazing hangers!" | "Crafted to last. Designed to be seen." |
| "Truly incredible quality you'll love" | "Woven by hand. Built for the long term." |
| "Shop now and transform your space!" | "The Collection" |

---

## By Channel

**Website:** Most restrained. Let white space and product imagery carry weight. Copy supports, not sells.

**Social:** Slightly warmer. Still minimal. Captions can be 1–3 lines or just a single phrase.

**Email:** Direct and personal. Subject lines under 8 words. Body copy under 150 words for campaigns.

**Ads:** 3–6 words max for headlines. Benefit-led, not hype-led.
