# Visual Strategy — Floki Marketing

This document is the marketing-side brief for all visual assets across Floki. It defines *what* we need and *why* — feeding directly into the design team's execution workflow in `Websites/design/`.

Design handles the *how*. Marketing owns the *what*.

---

## How This Works

1. Marketing identifies a visual need (campaign, page section, social content)
2. Marketing writes a visual brief here — mood, intent, product, placement
3. Design picks it up, runs it through the reference system and AI workflow
4. Output comes back to marketing for review against campaign intent
5. Approved assets go to dev for implementation

---

## Visual Pillars

These are the three visual identities Floki operates within. Every asset should map to one.

### 1. Editorial Minimal
Clean, considered, premium. White space does the work. Product is the hero.
- **Used for:** Product pages, hero sliders, collection banners
- **Mood:** Quiet luxury. Confidence without noise.
- **Reference direction:** Studio product photography, high-fashion lookbooks, Kinfolk-style home editorial

### 2. Lifestyle Context
Product in its natural environment. Real spaces, real moments.
- **Used for:** Our Story page, social content, email campaigns
- **Mood:** Aspirational but attainable. Warm, inhabited spaces.
- **Reference direction:** Apartment Therapy, minimal wardrobe content, African home decor editorial

### 3. Cultural Richness
Bold color, pattern, and texture — specifically for African print products.
- **Used for:** Pillow collection, campaign moments, cultural brand storytelling
- **Mood:** Proud, vivid, modern. Not costume — culture.
- **Reference direction:** Contemporary African design, Adire textile editorial, Afrofuturist visual culture

## Reference Image Pipeline

This is how marketing feeds the design team's reference-based workflow.

### Step 1 — Identify the need
Write a one-line brief: what product, what mood, what placement.

### Step 2 — Pull references
Use Pinterest. Search terms mapped to each pillar:

**Editorial Minimal:**
- `luxury product photography minimal`
- `editorial wardrobe photography`
- `studio hanger photography white background`
- `capsule wardrobe editorial`

**Lifestyle Context:**
- `minimal home lifestyle photography`
- `fashion accessory editorial desk`
- `sneaker lifestyle photography`
- `African home decor lifestyle`

**Cultural Richness:**
- `Ankara textile editorial photography`
- `African print home decor styled`
- `contemporary African design editorial`
- `Adire fabric close up photography`

### Step 3 — Brief the design team
Create an entry in the **Active Visual Briefs** section below with:
- Product
- Pillar
- Reference images (links or saved files)
- Placement on site
- Deadline

## Asset Review Checklist

Before any visual asset is approved by marketing:

- [ ] Maps to one of the three visual pillars
- [ ] Colors are compatible with brand palette (see `Websites/design/02-STYLE-GUIDE.md`)
- [ ] No obvious AI artifacts or distortions
- [ ] Composition has breathing room — not cluttered
- [ ] Feels like something Floki would actually publish
- [ ] File named using the convention in the Style Guide
