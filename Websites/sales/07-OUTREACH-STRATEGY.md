## Email Domain Warm-Up (Do This Before Any Mass Outreach)

**Why it matters:** A fresh Gmail or Google Workspace account sending 50+ cold emails per day will get flagged as spam. Domain reputation takes 2-4 weeks to build.

**Warm-up protocol for shopflokibrands@gmail.com:**

Week 1 (Days 1-7):
- Send 5 emails/day to real contacts (colleagues, friends, your own test accounts)
- Reply to some of those emails to build bidirectional engagement
- Subscribe to 5-10 industry newsletters and open them

Week 2 (Days 8-14):
- Increase to 20 emails/day
- Continue opening received emails
- Send some casual, non-sales emails to real people

Week 3 (Days 15-21):
- Increase to 40-50 emails/day
- Now safe to begin outreach to top A-tier prospects only (15 contacts)

Week 4+:
- Full outreach volume: 50-75 cold emails/day
- Cap at 100/day on Gmail to avoid rate limiting

**Deliverability best practices:**
- Always use a plain-text or minimal HTML email (no heavy images or logos in cold outreach)
- Personalize at minimum [First Name] and [Company]
- Avoid spam trigger words: "Free," "Guaranteed," "Buy now," "Limited time"
- Include a physical address in email footer (legally required for CAN-SPAM)
- Include an unsubscribe link in all bulk outreach
- Check sender score monthly at mail-tester.com (send a test email, aim for 9+/10)

## LinkedIn Outreach Strategy

LinkedIn is the second touch for A-tier prospects and the primary channel for contacts with no verified email.

### Finding Prospects on LinkedIn

Search operators:
- `"Purchasing Manager" "hotel" "New York"` in LinkedIn search
- `"Director of Operations" "vacation rental" "Los Angeles"`
- `"FF&E" "procurement" "hospitality"`
- `"Buyer" "boutique" "retail" "home goods"`

Use LinkedIn Sales Navigator (from $79/month) for:
- Advanced filters: company size, industry, seniority, geography
- "Changed jobs in last 90 days" filter - new leaders are vendor-agnostic
- Saved lead lists + alerts when contacts post

### LinkedIn Connection Request Message (250 char limit)

Hi [First Name], I'm reaching out from Floki Brands - we make luxury woven nylon hangers for boutique hotels and hospitality properties. Would love to connect and share what we do.

(For Stylists): Hi [First Name], love your work - especially [specific thing]. I make luxury hangers for closet installs. Would love to connect and share our trade program.

### LinkedIn Follow-Up Message (after connection accepted)

Hi [First Name], thanks for connecting. As mentioned, Floki Brands makes luxury woven nylon hangers on aluminum frames in five colorways - built specifically for [hotels / STR properties / boutique retail]. I'd love to send you a sample kit if you have 2 minutes to review our product. No pitch, just the product. Worth it?

## Phone Call Strategy (A-Tier Warm Leads Only)

Make phone calls only on Day 8 of the 5-touch sequence for A-tier prospects who have not responded to email or LinkedIn. This is not cold calling - it is a follow-up to prior email contact.

### Script: Hospitality (Hotel/FF&E/STR)

"Hi, this is [Name] calling from Floki Brands. I sent [First Name] an email about our luxury woven hangers last week - not sure if it got through. We supply premium OS&E to boutique hotels and STR operators, and I wanted to make sure you had a chance to see the product. I can email you a line sheet and sample kit in 60 seconds. Is this a good time for 2 minutes?"

If voicemail: "Hi, this is [Name] from Floki Brands - I sent you an email about our luxury woven hangers. I'd love to get 5 minutes on your calendar. I'll follow up via email. Thanks."

### Script: Boutique Retail

"Hi, this is [Name] from Floki Brands. I reached out about our wholesale hangers last week. We're a premium woven nylon hanger brand - 5 colors - great margin for boutique retail. I wanted to make sure you had the info. Can I send you a sample? Takes 2 seconds."

- Apollo.io includes direct dials for many contacts on Basic+ plans
- LinkedIn contact info often includes phone numbers
- Company websites often list direct numbers for purchasing departments

## Trade Show Strategy

Trade shows are investment events, not passive attendance. Have a plan for each.

### HD Expo (Hospitality Design Expo)
- **When:** May annually, Las Vegas
- **Why attend:** 10,000+ hospitality professionals. FF&E buyers, hotel operators, interior designers, procurement firms all attend.
- **Goal:** 50 business card exchanges, 10 sample kit handoffs, 5 follow-up meetings booked
- **Action:** Apply for 10x10 exhibitor booth (HD Expo 2027, apply by January 2027). Attendance badge is $500-1,500. Booth starts at $3,000.
- See [12-TRADE-SHOWS-AND-DIRECTORIES.md](./12-TRADE-SHOWS-AND-DIRECTORIES.md) for full details

### BDNY (Boutique Design New York)
- **When:** November annually, NYC (Javits Center)
- **Why attend:** The definitive boutique + lifestyle hotel design show. Every major boutique hotel buyer attends.
- **Target:** BDNY November 2026 - attend as exhibitor or badge attendee
- **Goal:** 30 meaningful conversations, 10 sample kit handoffs, intro to FF&E buyers

### The Lodging Conference
- **When:** September annually, Phoenix, AZ
- **Why attend:** Hotel owners, operators, developers - decision-makers, not designers. Best show for hotel direct wholesale and STR management outreach.
- **Badge:** $1,500-2,500 for attendee. Exhibitor tables available.

## Response Handling Rules

1. Respond to every warm lead within 24 hours. No exceptions.
2. First response to a warm lead: thank them, confirm their specific need (how many units? what type of property?), offer to send samples + line sheet.
3. For A-tier warm leads: propose a 15-minute call immediately.
4. For sample requests: confirm shipping address, note in HubSpot, ship within 48 hours.
5. All pricing questions answered with the exact terms from [09-WHOLESALE-TERMS.md](./09-WHOLESALE-TERMS.md). No improvising on price.
6. Any negotiation on pricing for orders above $2,000: escalate to Anthony before committing.
