# 12 - Trade Shows and Directories: Floki Brands

In-person trade show strategy and hospitality supplier directory registrations. Trade shows generate high-value leads impossible to reach via cold email. Directories create passive inbound from procurement teams actively searching for suppliers.

## Priority Trade Shows

### Tier 1 - Must Attend (Year 1)

**HD Expo (Hospitality Design Exposition & Conference)**
- Location: Mandalay Bay, Las Vegas, NV
- When: May annually (May 2027 for next cycle)
- What: The largest hospitality design trade show in North America. FF&E buyers, interior designers, hotel operators, and procurement directors all attend. This is the single highest-leverage event for Floki's B2B hotel channel.
- Attendees: 8,000+ hospitality professionals
- Booth cost: Small booth (10x10) starts at approximately $3,500-5,000. Shared/startup section may be available at lower cost.
- Total budget estimate: $7,000-12,000 (booth + travel + samples + display)
- ROI target: 20-50 qualified leads, 3-5 pilot orders ($5,000-25,000 pipeline value)
- How to exhibit: hdexpo.com - exhibitor registration typically opens 6-8 months before event
- Alternative: Attend as a visitor first ($500-800 registration) to scope the event, network, and plan a booth for Year 2

**BDNY (Boutique Design New York)**
- Location: Javits Center, New York, NY
- When: November annually
- What: Premier boutique and lifestyle hospitality design show. More targeted than HD Expo. Focused on independent, boutique, and design-forward properties. Perfect fit for Floki's positioning.
- Attendees: 3,000+ boutique hospitality buyers
- Booth cost: Startup/emerging brand section approximately $2,000-4,000 (10x10)
- Total budget estimate: $4,000-8,000 (lower since NYC is local - no flights/hotel)
- ROI target: 15-30 qualified leads, 2-4 pilot orders
- How to exhibit: bdny.com - applications open in spring
- NYC location makes this the most cost-effective show for Floki

### Tier 2 - Attend Year 1, Exhibit Year 2

**NY NOW**
- Location: Javits Center, New York, NY
- When: January and August annually (two shows per year)
- What: Major wholesale marketplace for home, lifestyle, and gift products. Strong DTC wholesale and boutique retail buyer attendance. Good for the retail and gifting channels.
- Attendees: 25,000+ retail buyers
- Booth cost: Approximately $2,500-5,000 depending on section
- Total budget: $5,000-10,000
- ROI target: 15-25 retail buyer leads, wholesale marketplace visibility
- How to exhibit: nynow.com
- Apply to the "Handmade" or "Home" section for best category placement

**International Home + Housewares Show**
- Location: McCormick Place, Chicago, IL
- When: March annually
- What: The largest home goods trade show in the US. Attracts major retail buyers (Target, Nordstrom, Bed Bath & Beyond successors, independent chains).
- Attendees: 50,000+
- Booth cost: $5,000-10,000 for small booth
- Total budget: $10,000-18,000
- ROI target: Retail buyer contacts, potential mass-market distribution conversations
- Note: Larger and more mass-market than ideal for Year 1. Better fit once Floki has traction and is ready to scale retail distribution.

### Tier 3 - Monitor and Evaluate

**The Lodging Conference**
- Location: Phoenix, AZ
- When: September/October annually
- What: Hotel owner and operator conference. Less design/procurement focused, more business/investment. Useful for meeting hotel group decision-makers.
- Attendees: 2,000+ hotel owners and operators
- Cost: $1,500-2,500 registration (no booth - networking event format)
- Best as: Networking/prospecting trip, not exhibit

**NEWH (Network of the Hospitality Industry) Regional Events**
- What: Regional networking events for hospitality design and procurement professionals throughout the year
- Cost: $50-200 per event
- Best as: Low-cost networking to build FF&E and designer relationships in local markets

## Trade Show Execution Playbook

### Before the Show (4-8 weeks out)
1. Register and confirm booth or attendance
2. Order sample display: mounted hangers in all 5 colors on a branded display board
3. Print materials: line sheet, business cards, one-page product spec
4. Pre-show outreach: email all prospects attending the show (check exhibitor/attendee lists) to schedule 15-min meetings at the booth
5. Set up a landing page or QR code for show-specific lead capture
6. Bring 50-100 sample hangers for giveaways (cost: $100-200)

### At the Show
1. Staff the booth with 1-2 people minimum (Anthony + one ops person)
2. Lead capture: use HubSpot mobile app to scan badges or capture contact info immediately
3. Photograph every meaningful interaction (permission first) for social content
4. Ask every lead: "What's your biggest frustration with your current hanger supplier?" - the answer tells you how to follow up
5. Collect business cards and note the conversation topic on the back immediately

### After the Show (within 72 hours)
1. Enter all leads into HubSpot CRM with source tag "TradeShow-[ShowName]-[Year]"
2. Send personalized follow-up email to every lead within 48 hours
3. Ship sample kits to hot leads within 1 week
4. Post show content on Instagram and LinkedIn
5. Report: leads captured, pipeline value created, cost per lead

## Supplier Directories

Registration on hospitality supplier directories creates passive inbound leads from procurement teams searching for products. Zero ongoing cost, potentially high-value leads.

### Hospitality-Specific Directories

**Avendra International - Supplier Portal**
- What: Group purchasing organization (GPO) for independent and boutique hotels. One of the largest in North America.
- Why: Getting listed means hotels on Avendra's platform can find and order Floki directly through their procurement system
- How to apply: avendra.com - navigate to "Become a Supplier" or contact supplier relations
- Cost: Free to apply. Avendra takes a commission on sales through the platform.
- Priority: HIGH - this is a multiplier for the hotel channel

**Entegra Procurement Services - Supplier Registration**
- What: GPO serving hotels, restaurants, and hospitality groups
- How to apply: entegra.com - supplier registration portal
- Cost: Free to apply
- Priority: HIGH

**Hotel Depot - Vendor Application**
- What: Direct supplier marketplace for hotels nationally. Already has a "cloth hangers" category.
- How to apply: thehoteldepot.com - vendor/supplier application
- Cost: Free to apply
- Priority: HIGH - existing category means buyers are already searching for this product

**HotelSupplies.com / Hospitality Marketplace Directories**
- What: Various online directories where hotel buyers search for products
- Register on: hotelsupplies.com, hotelinteriors.com, hospitalitynet.org supplier directory
- Cost: Varies (some free, some paid listings $200-500/year)
- Priority: MEDIUM

**Hotel Tech Report - Supplier Profile**
- What: Review and comparison platform for hotel technology and supplies
- Why: Creates SEO-indexed profile that procurement teams find via Google
- How: hoteltechreport.com - create a vendor profile
- Cost: Free basic profile, paid premium listings available
- Priority: MEDIUM

### Design Industry Directories

**Boutique Design (BD) - Supplier Directory**
- What: Online directory associated with BDNY trade show
- How: Submit via boutiquedesign.com
- Priority: HIGH - direct connection to BDNY attendees

**NEWH - Supplier/Partner Directory**
- What: Network of the Hospitality Industry member directory
- How: newh.org - join as a supplier member ($200-500/year)
- Priority: MEDIUM - good for networking with hospitality designers

**Houzz Pro - Trade Program**
- What: Directory for trade professionals to discover products
- How: houzz.com/trade - register as a trade product supplier
- Priority: MEDIUM - reaches interior designers and staging companies

### Wholesale Marketplaces (also in [10-RETAILER-TARGETS.md](./10-RETAILER-TARGETS.md))

- Faire (faire.com) - largest wholesale marketplace, free to list
- Tundra (tundra.com) - no commission model, free shipping
- Abound (helloabound.com) - curated independent retail
- Bulletin (bulletin.co) - DTC brands selling wholesale
