# Content Strategy — Floki Marketing

Content pillars, formats, cadence, and production workflow. Content is how the brand thesis becomes visible at scale.

Read [01-BRAND-THESIS.md](./01-BRAND-THESIS.md) and [05-BRAND-VOICE.md](./05-BRAND-VOICE.md) before producing any content.

## Floki Content Pillars

Every piece of content maps to one of four pillars. These are not content types — they are the four stories the brand tells repeatedly.

| Pillar | What It Tells | Audience |
|---|---|---|
| **Craft** | The product itself — materials, process, detail. What makes it worth it. | DTC + Trade |
| **Context** | The life our customer lives. The spaces Floki belongs in. | DTC primary |
| **Culture** | The heritage and design language behind the African print line. | DTC + cultural audience |
| **Proof** | Real use, real spaces, real people. Validation. | DTC + Trade |

**Starting ratio:** 35% Context / 30% Craft / 20% Culture / 15% Proof

The ratio shifts as the brand matures. At launch, Craft and Context build the foundation. Proof builds as product ships. Culture deepens as the pillow line gets more attention.

## Formats by Channel

### Instagram

| Format | Pillar | Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| Static grid post | Craft / Context | 3–4x per week |
| Reel (15–30s) | Context / Culture | 2x per week |
| Story | Proof / Craft detail | Daily when active |
| Carousel | Craft detail / multi-product | 1x per week |

**Grid aesthetic:** Alternating product and lifestyle shots. No back-to-back product posts. Color stays within the visual system — no jarring temperature shifts between posts.

### TikTok

| Format | Pillar | Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| Product showcase | Craft | 2–3x per week |
| Lifestyle / vibe clip | Context | 2x per week |
| Cultural story | Culture | 1x per week |

**TikTok note:** Slightly more direct than Instagram. Trends are fine where they fit the brand naturally — never forced. If you have to stretch the brand to fit the trend, skip the trend.

### Email

| Type | Purpose | Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| Launch email | Introduce the brand, drive first visit | Once at launch |
| Product spotlight | Single product, single focus | Bi-weekly |
| Brand story | Craft narrative, cultural depth | Monthly |
| Abandoned cart | Recover lost sales | Automated |
| Welcome series | New subscriber onboarding | 2–3 emails automated |

**Email rules:** Subject lines under 8 words. Body under 150 words for campaigns. One CTA per email. No emoji in subject lines.

## Content Calendar Rhythm

**Weekly:**
- Mon: Plan the week's posts, confirm creative is ready
- Tue–Thu: Publish primary content
- Fri: Review performance, schedule weekend content
- Weekend: Light — Stories and reposts only

**Monthly:**
- Review top-performing content across channels
- Update [10-ANALYTICS.md](./10-ANALYTICS.md) with results
- Plan next month's campaign content
- Brief design on new visual needs in [06-VISUAL-IDENTITY.md](./06-VISUAL-IDENTITY.md)

---

## Trade Content

Content directed at trade audiences lives at the intersection of Craft and Proof. The audience is evaluating — content should support evaluation, not create aspiration.

**Priority assets for trade:**
- Hotel wardrobe context photography (proof shot)
- Product specification detail imagery
- Testimonials or case studies from hotel/STR partners (as relationships develop)

These assets serve double duty: they function as trade content and reinforce DTC credibility.
