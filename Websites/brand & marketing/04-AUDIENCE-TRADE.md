# Audience — Trade / B2B

Floki's institutional buyer segments. Five distinct segments, each with its own decision-making logic.

See [03-AUDIENCE-DTC.md](./03-AUDIENCE-DTC.md) for direct-to-consumer profiles.
For sales prospect lists, outreach, and pipeline: see `Websites/sales/`.

## Segment 1 — FF&E Procurement Firms

The highest-leverage B2B segment. One firm can represent 100–500+ units across multiple hotel properties in a single order.

**Profile**
- Purchasing agents and project managers at firms sourcing all physical goods for hotel openings and renovations
- Evaluates on specification compliance, scalability, and aesthetics
- Rarely the final decision-maker — reports to designers and hotel brand directors
- Moves on project cycles, not continuously
- Values proven product and easy supplier relationships above all

**What Motivates the Decision**
1. Consistent quality across volume. Can every unit in a 300-piece order be identical?
2. Aesthetic that survives the design review. Will the interior designer approve it?
3. Durability. Hotel environments are hard on products.
4. Price within project budget. Volume pricing tiers matter here.
5. Reliable supplier relationship. Fast samples, responsive comms, clean ordering process.

**How to Reach Them**
- Direct outreach to VP Purchasing or Procurement Director
- Sample kit is non-negotiable — no specification without product in hand
- Initial email qualifies; the actual sell happens on a call

**Copy approach:** Professional and concise. Lead with capability and specs. Trade buyers do not want to be marketed to — they want to evaluate. Give them what they need to make a decision.

## Segment 2 — Hotel Direct (Boutique and Lifestyle Properties)

Direct hotel purchasing — GMs, Directors of Operations, property buyers at design-forward boutique properties.

**Profile**
- Boutique and lifestyle hotels (Ace, Arlo, Proper, independents)
- Care about design intent — these properties chose to be boutique for a reason
- Lower volume than FF&E firms but faster decision cycles and more direct relationships
- The GM or DOO frequently has strong aesthetic opinions and is the actual decision-maker
- Responsive to brands that demonstrate understanding of their world

**What Motivates the Decision**
1. Aesthetic alignment. Does this fit what we're trying to be?
2. Guest experience impact. Will guests notice? Will they photograph it?
3. Durability under hospitality conditions. Laundry cycles, daily guest use.
4. Clear volume pricing. They are not comparing on price — they are qualifying on value.

**How to Reach Them**
- Direct outreach to GM or Director of Operations
- Lead with: "Other boutique properties like yours have switched because guests notice the difference."
- Visual context matters — show the product in a hotel wardrobe environment

**Copy approach:** More conversational than FF&E, still professional. Speak to the property's brand values. The hook is guest experience quality, not cost savings.

## Segment 3 — STR Management Companies

Short-term rental operators managing premium property portfolios at scale.

**Profile**
- Companies managing 50–5,000+ premium Airbnb/VRBO-listed properties
- Purchase in bulk for consistent guest experience across portfolios
- Two primary concerns: guest review scores and operational efficiency
- Faster decision cycles than traditional hotels — smaller teams, less bureaucracy
- Growing rapidly at the premium end — this is a market in expansion

**What Motivates the Decision**
1. Guest review impact. Will this improve star ratings?
2. Visual differentiation. Guests photograph the spaces they stay in. Floki hangers appear in those photos.
3. Consistent quality at volume. Every unit across 200 properties needs to match.
4. Scale pricing. Volume tiers matter at this segment's purchasing volume.

**How to Reach Them**
- VP Operations or Purchasing/Supply Chain Manager
- Frame around: "The details guests photograph and mention in reviews."
- Instagram-friendly aesthetics are a genuine operational advantage for STR operators

## Segment 4 — Boutique Retail

Independent boutiques and curated retail stores.

**Profile**
- Owner-operated or small buying team with strong aesthetic identity
- Curates to a specific customer and will reject anything that breaks the room
- Moves on intuition and visual fit more than data
- Values discovering something before it is everywhere
- Open to consignment or low MOQ terms for new brands

**What Motivates the Decision**
1. Aesthetic fit with the store's identity.
2. The story. Can the buyer explain to a customer why this is special?
3. Brand alignment — are they comfortable putting their curatorial reputation behind it?
4. Workable terms. Net-30 or consignment for new relationships is standard.

**How to Reach Them**
- Personal email or in-person where possible
- Show, don't tell — lead with visuals
- Have a line sheet ready but do not open with it

**Target retailers:** Flying Solo NYC, Des Kohan, En Avance, Swan Space, Virtu

## Segment 5 — Personal Stylists

Wardrobe professionals with high-trust client relationships and direct referral power.

**Profile**
- Personal stylists and closet consultants
- High-trust relationships with clients who invest in their wardrobes
- No inventory risk — trade pricing with referral commission removes all friction
- They are the trusted recommendation layer between Floki and high-value individual buyers
- Many have significant Instagram audiences — secondary marketing surface

**What Motivates the Decision**
1. Product quality. Their professional reputation is attached to every recommendation.
2. Commission structure. 15–20% referral on client orders.
3. Ease of recommendation. Can they explain it in one sentence? Can they send a direct link?
4. Aesthetic credibility. Would they use it in their own home?

**How to Reach Them**
- Direct DM on Instagram is as effective as email for this segment
- Lead with: trade pricing, no minimums, referral commission structure
- Zero friction to try — that is the whole pitch
