# Brand Positioning — Floki

Where Floki sits in the market, what it owns, and how it is differentiated from everything around it. This is the strategic architecture that all marketing executes against.

Read [01-BRAND-THESIS.md](./01-BRAND-THESIS.md) first. Positioning is the external expression of the internal conviction.

---

## The Category We Are Building

Floki does not compete in "hangers" or "home decor" or "phone accessories."

Floki is building the category of **considered everyday objects** — a premium-accessible brand for the products people live with daily but rarely think about. This category does not yet exist as a named market. That is the opportunity.

---

## Positioning Statement

For style-conscious individuals and hospitality professionals who believe that the objects you live with daily deserve the same care as the ones you display, Floki offers considered everyday objects — defined by craft, intention, and the conviction that quality belongs in every corner of a space, not just the visible ones.

Unlike commodity suppliers who treat functional objects as afterthoughts, Floki treats every product as a design decision. Unlike luxury brands that use price as a signal, Floki uses craft.

---

## The Two-Market Structure as Brand Architecture

Floki operates in two markets simultaneously. This is not a distribution strategy. It is a brand architecture decision.

**DTC:** Style-conscious individuals purchasing for personal use. The brand role here is aspirational but accessible — "designed for spaces that demand more" means their space qualifies.

**Trade / Hospitality:** Hotels, FF&E firms, STR operators purchasing for guest environments. The brand role here is professional standard — if it passes the hotel buyer's standard, it is credible.

The asymmetry that creates brand power: the trade buyer purchases at volume and demands quality. The DTC buyer encounters the brand in a hotel and trusts it. This is the validation loop that luxury brands spend decades manufacturing. Floki builds it into its market architecture from launch.

## Competitive Landscape

### Hanger Market

| Player | Position | Gap Floki Fills |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| Huggable Hangers | Mass-market functional | No aesthetic, no brand |
| Zara Home / CB2 hangers | Retailer private label | Incidental product, no story |
| Hotel bulk suppliers | B2B commodity | No DTC presence, no design identity |
| Premium wood hanger brands | Craft luxury | High price, no color, no cultural dimension |

**Floki's position:** The only hanger brand with aesthetic identity, a story, and simultaneous DTC and hospitality presence. Uncontested.

### Decorative Pillow Market

| Player | Position | Gap Floki Fills |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| Target / IKEA | Mass-market commodity | No craft, no story |
| West Elm / CB2 | Mid-premium home decor | Generic aesthetic, no cultural depth |
| Etsy artisan shops | Cultural/handmade | No brand architecture, no scale |
| African lifestyle fashion brands | Cultural fashion primary | Home is secondary market for them |

**Floki's position:** African print pillow design executed with premium brand standards. Cultural craft elevated to brand level — not ethnic decor, not generic.

### Phone Case Market (Sneaker Cases)

| Player | Position | Gap Floki Fills |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| CASETiFY | Designer collab fashion | Mass marketing, seasonal rotation |
| OtterBox / Speck | Protection-primary utility | Zero aesthetic |
| Sneaker culture micro-brands | Community-specific | No premium positioning |

**Floki's position:** Sneaker culture meets considered design. Protection as premium object, not commodity.

## What Floki Owns

**The everyday object white space.** No one is building brand identity around products people use daily but overlook. Floki owns this positioning.

**Quality without performance.** Floki does not make loud claims. Product quality and the hospitality signal do the work. The brand is confident, not assertive.

**Cultural + premium in the same sentence.** The African print collection proves that elevated design carries heritage — and that this is a market position, not a niche.

**The hospitality validation signal.** "Used by boutique hotels" is ownable at this price point and no competitor can replicate it.

---

## Pricing Architecture

Floki is premium-accessible. Not luxury. Not commodity.

| Tier | Signal | Floki's Place |
|------|--------|---------------|
| Luxury | Price as exclusion signal | Above Floki |
| Premium-accessible | Craft as quality signal | **Floki** |
| Mid-market | Price-performance tradeoff | Below Floki |
| Commodity | Price-primary | Far below Floki |

DTC retail pricing — $9.99 single, $34.99 four-pack, $79.99 eight-pack — sits clearly in the premium-accessible tier. The pack discounts reward deliberate purchasing, which is behaviorally consistent with the brand's customer.

Wholesale pricing across B2B segments maintains a minimum 47% gross margin at all volume tiers, with pricing structured to reward scale (hotel volume tiers from $5.50 down to $3.75 at 500+ units). See `Websites/sales/07-WHOLESALE-TERMS.md` for full pricing.

## The Brand Promise

Every Floki product will be better-looking, better-made, and more considered than anything else at its price point in its category.

That is the standard everything is measured against.

---

## Product Line as Brand Expression

The three product lines are not sub-brands. They are three expressions of the same conviction.

- **Woven Hangers** — organization as object. The wardrobe's daily companion, treated as a design decision.
- **African Print Pillows** — culture as object. Heritage and craft elevated to premium home goods.
- **Sneaker Cases** — care as object. Protection for the things you love, built like something you'd want to be seen with.

Each product asks the same question: what happens when you treat a functional object with the same care you'd give a featured one?
