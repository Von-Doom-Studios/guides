# Brand Voice — Floki

How Floki communicates across every surface. Read [01-BRAND-THESIS.md](./01-BRAND-THESIS.md) before writing anything. Voice is the thesis made audible.

---

## The Core Principle

Every piece of copy should feel like it earns the line: **"Designed for spaces that demand more."**

That means: premium without arrogance. Confident without noise. It does not mean formal. It means considered.

---

## Tone in One Line

**Short. Specific. Earned.**

Short because confidence does not need to explain itself. Specific because vague claims are a sign of an unsure brand. Earned because the copy should feel like the product deserves it — not like marketing.

---

## The Rules

**Write short sentences.** No run-ons. No compound thought chains. If a sentence has more than two clauses, break it.

**Cut filler words.** "Incredibly," "amazing," "truly," "really," "just," "very" — all gone. They weaken the sentence they are trying to strengthen.

**No exclamation marks.** Ever. If the copy needs an exclamation mark to generate enthusiasm, the copy is not doing its job.

**No clichés.** "Elevate your space." "Where quality meets style." "Luxury redefined." "The perfect gift." These are the first phrases that come to mind — which is exactly why they do not belong here.

**No corporate speak.** Write like a person with taste, not a press release. "We believe in crafting premium..." is not a sentence a real person would say.

**Never salesy.** State facts. Let the product sell itself. The brand does not persuade — it shows.

**No em dashes.** Use a hyphen or restructure the sentence.

---

## DTC Voice

Speak to someone who notices. Do not explain. Do not sell. Describe.

The DTC buyer has already decided they care about this — your job is to confirm that this is the right choice for someone with their standard.

See [03-AUDIENCE-DTC.md](./03-AUDIENCE-DTC.md) for full buyer profiles.

| Instead of... | Write... |
|---|---|
| "Elevate your home with our amazing hangers!" | "Crafted to last. Designed to be seen." |
| "Truly incredible quality you'll love" | "Woven by hand. Built for the long term." |
| "Shop now and transform your space!" | "The Collection" |
| "Perfect for any home or hotel!" | "From a private wardrobe to a boutique hotel suite." |
| "Our beautiful African print pillows celebrate culture" | "A design language that has existed for centuries. Finally in your living room." |

---

## Trade / B2B Voice

The trade buyer does not want to be marketed to. They want to evaluate.

Lead with capability. Be direct about specs, pricing, and terms. Warmth is fine — pressure is not. One clear ask per communication.

See [04-AUDIENCE-TRADE.md](./04-AUDIENCE-TRADE.md) for full trade segment profiles.

| Instead of... | Write... |
|---|---|
| "We'd love to partner with your amazing hotel!" | "Floki hangers are in production now with 1,000 units available. We offer volume pricing starting at 50 units." |
| "Our premium product would elevate your guests' experience!" | "Guests notice the wardrobe. These are the hangers that hold up to daily hotel use and still look considered." |
| "We think your boutique is a perfect fit for our brand." | "Your buyers tend to look for things that have a story behind them. Here's ours." |

## By Channel

**Website:** Most restrained. Let white space and product photography carry weight. Copy supports — it does not sell.

**Social (Instagram / TikTok):** Slightly warmer. Still minimal. Captions can be 1–3 lines or just a single phrase. On TikTok, can be more direct. Never forced into trends.

**Email:** Direct and personal. Subject lines under 8 words. Body copy under 150 words for campaigns. One CTA per email.

**Paid ads:** 3–6 words for headlines. Benefit-led, not hype-led. The visual does the heavy lifting.

**Trade outreach:** Professional, not stiff. One clear ask. No deck language. See email templates in `Websites/sales/06-EMAIL-TEMPLATES.md`.

---

## Per-Product Voice Notes

**Woven Hangers**
Lead with craft and function in equal measure. The color is a feature — name it. "Canary yellow woven nylon" is a sentence that earns itself. Do not hide the functional superiority behind aesthetics.

**African Print Pillows**
The cultural dimension is the product. Do not treat it as a feature to mention — it is the reason the product exists. Write with the same confidence you would give any premium design piece. Do not explain the culture — describe the object.

**Sneaker Cases**
The buyer cares about the sneaker first. The case is protection for something they love. Lead with care and preservation, not protection specs.

---

## What the Voice Is Not

Not precious. Floki is confident, not self-important.

Not cold. Minimal does not mean distant. There is warmth in precision.

Not explaining itself. A brand with conviction does not need to justify its existence in every sentence.
