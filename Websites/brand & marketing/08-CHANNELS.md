# Channels — Floki Marketing

Channel-by-channel strategy, ownership, and approach for Floki.

Read [03-AUDIENCE.md](./03-AUDIENCE.md) before planning channel-specific content. Every channel has a primary audience — speak to that audience specifically.

## Instagram

**Handle:** TBD

**Primary audience:** The Considerate Buyer (28–42, DTC, style-conscious)

**Goal:** Build brand awareness and visual credibility. Drive traffic to the Shopify store.

**Content approach:** Editorial Minimal and Lifestyle Context pillars do most of the work. Cultural Richness pillar surfaces when the pillow collection is prominent. Captions follow brand voice — short, no filler.

**Grid strategy:** Alternating product and lifestyle. Consistent color temperature across the feed. No back-to-back product posts. The grid as a whole should feel like a designed object.

**Key tactics:**
- Reels prioritized for reach
- Carousels for product detail and saves (saves are a strong signal of purchase intent)
- Stories for real-time engagement and proof content
- Link in bio routed to Shopify store

---

## TikTok

**Primary audience:** DTC discovery — catches the considerate buyer earlier in the journey

**Goal:** Product discovery. Reach the younger end of the target demographic before Instagram does.

**Content approach:** Direct product showcases, lifestyle clips, cultural moments. Slightly more personality than Instagram — still within the brand voice, never cringe.

**Rule:** If a trend doesn't fit Floki naturally, skip it. The brand does not chase.

---

## Email

**Platform recommendation:** Klaviyo (native Shopify integration, flow builder, segmentation)

**List building:** Newsletter signup in site footer. Offer TBD — consider early access, first purchase discount, or a brand editorial piece as the hook.

**Segmentation (planned):**
- All subscribers
- Purchasers by product category
- Abandoned cart
- VIP / repeat purchasers

**Flows to build (priority order):**
1. Welcome series — 2–3 emails introducing the brand and product range
2. Abandoned cart — 1–2 emails
3. Post-purchase follow-up
4. Win-back — inactive subscribers at 90 days

**Campaign cadence:** See [07-CONTENT-STRATEGY.md](./07-CONTENT-STRATEGY.md)

## Pinterest

**Primary audience:** Passive discovery — the considerate buyer who is not yet looking for Floki, but will recognize it

**Goal:** Long-term SEO-driven traffic. Pinterest is a discovery engine for home decor and lifestyle with a longer traffic tail than Instagram.

**Approach:** Pin every product image and lifestyle image with keyword-rich descriptions. Pinterest works through volume and keyword density — this is a slow-build channel.

**Boards:**
- Floki Home
- Woven Hanger Inspiration
- African Print Home Decor
- Sneaker Accessories
- Minimal Living
- Boutique Hotel Details

---

## Paid — Meta (Instagram / Facebook)

**Status:** Pre-launch planning

**When to activate:** After organic presence is established and product photography is strong. Do not run paid ads against weak creative.

**Priority campaign sequence:**
1. Launch awareness — lifestyle creative, broad interest targeting (home decor, fashion, boutique hotels)
2. Retargeting — website visitors and abandoned cart
3. Product-specific — hanger, pillow, case to relevant interest segments

**Creative approach:** Lead with top-performing organic creative. Always test two variants minimum. Visual-first — never text-heavy. The DTC considerate buyer will scroll past anything that looks like an ad trying too hard.

**KPIs:** ROAS, CTR, cost per purchase. See [09-ANALYTICS.md](./09-ANALYTICS.md).

---

## LinkedIn (Trade / B2B)

**Primary audience:** FF&E buyers, hotel operations professionals, boutique retail buyers

**Goal:** B2B brand visibility. Not a primary channel — but useful for establishing credibility with trade buyers who will look you up before responding to outreach.

**Content:** Hotel wardrobe context imagery, brand story, any press or trade coverage as it develops.

**Cadence:** Opportunistic — no fixed cadence until the DTC channels are running consistently.
